Identity

Who We Are

Jump Ship Quantum Acquisitions is the premier dealer of scientifically impossible artifacts and substances. We are a team of quantum physicists, temporal engineers, and interdimensional traders who have dedicated our existence to sourcing items that defy conventional understanding of physics and reality.

Founded in 2147, we operate from our orbital station at Lagrange Point L5, where the laws of physics are more... flexible. Our network spans multiple dimensions, timelines, and quantum states, allowing us to acquire items that simply shouldn't exist according to standard scientific models.

We serve collectors, researchers, and institutions that require access to materials and artifacts that push the boundaries of what is considered possible. Every item in our catalog has been verified through rigorous quantum authentication protocols and comes with full documentation of its impossible properties.

Timeline

Our History

2147 — The Beginning

Jump Ship was founded by Dr. Elena Voss and Commander Marcus Chen after their breakthrough discovery of a stable quantum entanglement network connecting parallel dimensions. This discovery opened the door to acquiring items that exist in theoretical spaces.

2152 — First Major Acquisition

We successfully acquired our first antimatter containment cell, proving that impossible items could be safely transported and stored. This milestone established our reputation as the most reliable dealer in the quantum acquisition market.

2158 — Orbital Station L5

We established our permanent headquarters at Lagrange Point L5, taking advantage of the unique gravitational and quantum properties of this location. The station serves as both our storage facility and interdimensional gateway.

2165 — Present Day

Today, Jump Ship Quantum Acquisitions is the leading authority on impossible artifacts. We've expanded our catalog to include temporal stabilizers, exotic matter crystals, zero-point energy modules, and countless other items that challenge our understanding of reality itself.
